Wondering what it look like to be Electronic Engineer?

“At its heart engineering is about using science to find creative practical solutions. It’s a noble profession.” – Queen Elizabeth II

  • Occupation
  • Electrical - Research engineer
  • Electronics - Design engineer
  • Power - Distribution engineer
  • Telecommunications providers – mobile phones, radio, TV and satellite companies
  • Manufacturers and providers of PCs, tablets and ATM machines
  • Scientific research – acoustics, optics, physics and nanotechnology
  • Medical device and medical instrument manufacturers
  • Aviation and aerospace companies
  • Integrated circuit layout designer
  • Electronics Engineer (Applications / Hardware )
  • The manufacturing sector – programmable logic controls (PLCs) and industrial machinery developers
  • Task
  • Design electronic components, software, products, or systems for commercial, industrial, medical, military, or scientific applications
  • Develop maintenance and testing procedures for electronic components and equipment
  • Evaluate systems and recommend design modifications or equipment repair
  • Inspect electronic equipment, instruments, and systems to make sure they meet safety standards and applicable regulations
  • Design electronic components, software, products, or systems for commercial, industrial, medical, military, or scientific applications
  • Plan and develop applications and modifications for electronic properties used in parts and systems in order to improve technical performance
  • Skills
  • Complex Problem Solving - Identifying complex problems and reviewing related information to develop and evaluate options and implement solutions.
  • Critical Thinking - Using logic and reasoning to identify the strengths and weaknesses of alternative solutions, conclusions or approaches to problems.
  • Troubleshooting - Determining causes of operating errors and deciding what to do about it.
  • Speaking - Talking to others to convey information effectively.
  • Operations Analysis - Analyzing needs and product requirements to create a design.
  • AutoDesk AutoCAD
  • Matlab
  • Mathematics - Using mathematics to solve problems.
